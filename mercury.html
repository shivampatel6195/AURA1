Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and is closest to the Sun. Despite its small size, Mercury has a big story to tell, with an extreme climate and a surface marked by craters and high cliffs. This planet is unique in its lack of atmosphere, leading to extreme temperature variations. Temperatures can soar to 430°C (800°F) during the day and plummet to -180°C (-290°F) at night.

Mercury's orbit is elliptical, and its distance from the Sun varies from 46 million kilometers (29 million miles) at its closest to 70 million kilometers (43 million miles) at its farthest. This proximity makes it challenging for scientists to observe it, as the Sun's glare often overwhelms its appearance in telescopes. However, missions such as NASA's MESSENGER have provided valuable insights.

With its incredibly long day and short year—Mercury takes 59 Earth days to rotate once but only 88 days to orbit the Sun—it presents a fascinating contrast to our home planet.
