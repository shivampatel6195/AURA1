Venus

Venus, often referred to as Earth's sister planet, is the second planet from the Sun. It is similar in size, mass, and composition to Earth, but its environment is vastly different. Venus has an incredibly thick atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, making it the hottest planet in the solar system with surface temperatures reaching up to 465°C (870°F).

Unlike most planets, Venus rotates in the opposite direction to its orbit, meaning that the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east. A day on Venus (one rotation) is longer than a year, taking about 243 Earth days to rotate once but only 225 days to orbit the Sun.

Despite its scorching heat, Venus has been a target of numerous missions. The Soviet Union’s Venera missions in the 1960s and 1970s provided the first close-up images of Venus' surface. In recent years, missions like NASA's Parker Solar Probe have provided new insights into Venus' atmosphere and magnetic field.
