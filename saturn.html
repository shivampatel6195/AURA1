Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system, known for its spectacular ring system. The rings are primarily composed of ice particles, rock debris, and dust, and they stretch over 280,000 kilometers (175,000 miles) wide.

Saturn is a gas giant, much like Jupiter, and is composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. The planet's atmosphere features beautiful bands of clouds and swirling storms, similar to Jupiter's. It takes Saturn about 29.5 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.

Saturn has 83 known moons, with the largest being Titan. Titan is the second-largest moon in the solar system and is known for its thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane. Other moons of Saturn, such as Enceladus, have shown evidence of water-ice plumes, sparking interest in the possibility of subsurface oceans and the potential for life.
